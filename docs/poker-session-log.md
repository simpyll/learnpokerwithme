## Strategic Roadmap: Climbing the Online 6-Max Microstakes

Transitioning through the ranks of online No Limit Hold'em requires a disciplined fusion of volume and capital management. The primary objective of this progression is to validate a sustainable win rate while maintaining a mathematical buffer against variance. 

The following roadmap outlines the technical parameters for moving from 2NL to the threshold of 25NL.
### Phase 1: Statistical Validation
To confirm a definitive edge over the field, a sample size of **10,000 hands per stake** serves as the baseline diagnostic. While this sample size remains subject to short-term variance, it provides sufficient data to identify core mechanical leaks and establish an estimated win rate measured in big blinds per 100 hands (bb/100).
 * **Target Win Rate:** 5.0 to 10.0 bb/100.
 * **Success Metric:** Positive expected value (+EV) over the 10k hand duration.
### Phase 2: Bankroll Requirements and Projections
The strategy utilizes an initial seed of **$450**, supplemented by an external contingency fund of **$700**. This creates a total potential capitalization of **$1,150**, ensuring the ability to withstand a 50-buy-in downswing at lower tiers.
The following table projects capital growth based on a conservative 5 bb/100 win rate over 10,000 hands at each tier:
| Stake Level | Hand Sample | Profit (5 bb/100) | Cumulative Bankroll* |
|---|---|---|---|
| **2NL ($0.01/$0.02)** | 10,000 | $10.00 | $460.00 |
| **5NL ($0.02/$0.05)** | 10,000 | $25.00 | $485.00 |
| **10NL ($0.05/$0.10)** | 10,000 | $50.00 | $535.00 |
| **25NL ($0.10/$0.25)** | — | — | Entry Point |
**Calculated using initial $450 deposit; does not include external $700 contingency.*
### Phase 3: Risk Management and Level Up
Advancing to the next stake occurs only when both the hand sample is completed and the bankroll meets the buy-in requirements for the subsequent level.
 1. **Conservative Padding:** Maintain a minimum of 40–50 buy-ins for the current stake.
 2. **The "Move Down" Rule:** If the bankroll drops below the 30-buy-in threshold for the current level, an immediate demotion to the previous stake is mandatory to preserve capital.
 3. **Deployment of External Funds:** The additional $700 serves as a professional safety net, allowing for aggressive shot-taking at 25NL once the 10NL proficiency is established.
This systematic approach prioritizes long-term sustainability over rapid, high-risk ascension. By the conclusion of the 10NL phase, the total bankroll (inclusive of winnings and external capital) will exceed **$1,200**, providing approximately 50 buy-ins for a robust start at 25NL.
